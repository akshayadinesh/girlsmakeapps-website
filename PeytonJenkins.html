Intro to Dragons

A dragon comes in many forms all around the world, some are small, h u g e, winged, scaly, feathered, and more. Generally they are beasts that have large, reptilian bodies, usually with arms and legs like that of a dog or a salamander. Eastern dragons look more like serpents, western ones look like giant winged dogs, and they come in a wide range inbetween . Images below can give examples of depictions of dragons:

Land Dragons

Land dragons are dragons that... well.... live on land. They can have wings and fly, but find food at low-atitiudes and can easily walk on ground.

Ocean dragons

Ocean dragons are dragons that live in the ocean and other large bodies of water. This name was given to them becaue the first described water dragons were from the ocean. They usually dawn finns and gills, or they can just hold their breath for a long time while they find underwater prey. Some of these dragons can walk on land but they spend most or all of theit time in large bodies of water. Could the Loch Ness Moster be an ocean dragon?

Amphibian Dragons

Amphibian dragons are exactly what they soundlike. A dragon that can live both on water and land. This group of dragons usually isn't as big as the others. Amphibian dragons can be thought of a large frogs and toads with wings.

Aerial Dragons

These dragons live at high altitiudes and rarely make contact with the ground. They have big wings and aren't as strong as the other kinds of dragons becasue they'd just have to swoop down to catch food.
